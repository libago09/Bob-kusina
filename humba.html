Humba is a popular Filipino dish that originated in the Visayan region of the Philippines.
It is a savory and sweet stew made with pork belly, soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, garlic, and black beans.
The dish is slow-cooked until the pork is tendee and the sauce is thick and flavorful.



HUMBA RECIPE

Ingredients

Name Qty.
Pork Belly 1 kg
Cloves of Garlic 5
Medium Onion 1
Pineapple Juice 240ml
Soy Sauce 1/2 Cup
Sugar 1Tbsp
Dried Bay Leaves 5
Black Beans 2 Tbsp
Water 1 Cup

STEPS

  • Step 1: Prepare the pork belly and cut them into serving portions. Add them to a heated pan (no oil) and then sear for a few minutes. Flip them to cook the other side. When the meat has browned and the oil has been rendered, set them aside.

  • Step 2: Remove some of the oil from the pan. Add the garlic and onions and saute for a few minutes. Add the seared pork back to the pan and saute them.

  • Step 3: Pour the pineapple juice and the soy sauce. Add some black pepper, sugar, dried bay leaves, and banna blossoms.

  • Step 4: Pour 1 to 2 cups of water to help cook the meat. Cover the pan and let it simmer for 45 to 60 minutes or until the pork is tender. Stir in between cooking to prevent burning.

  • Step 5: After an hour, add the vinegar. Do not stir of 3 minutes. Wait for the raw taste of the vinegar to evaporate before stirring. Then, add the black beans. Cover again and let it cook for a few minutes until the sauce has been reduced. Turn the heat off.

  • Step 6: Transfer the pork humba to a serving plate. Enjoy this together with a big bowl of steaming rice. Yum!
